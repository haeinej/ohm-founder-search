# On February 20th, I started building ohm.

On February 20th, I started building ohm.

By March 20th, it was live in the App Store. 30 days from idea to execution.

But building the app wasn’t the hard part.

In 2025, anyone can build a functional app with Claude Code in a weekend. Execution is becoming a commodity.

Here is what I learned about what actually matters when the &quotbuilding&quot becomes easy:

1. Solve your own frustration. I’m a paid LinkedIn user, but the UI makes it impossible to do the one thing I want: find interesting people outside my network. I stopped complaining about the UI and started building a different way to connect. That became ohm.

2. Execution is cheap. Courage is scarce.Last week, I flew to New York with 100 printed flyers. I walked up to strangers and sold them a product that barely worked.

Most didn't care. But over dozens of conversations, something shifted. I stopped pitching the product and started reading the person.

3. Sales is a survival skill, not a department. I used to think: &quotIf it’s good enough, people will find it.&quot That’s just arrogance dressed up as integrity.

If you can build an app through Claude Code in hours, your code isn't your moat. Your ability to understand the user’s soul is your moat. Marketing isn't &quotselling out&quot—it's the bridge between your intention and their behavior.

4. Stop selling. Start inviting.I was pushing too hard to &quotget users.&quot When I changed my pitch to: &quotYou’re not a user, you’re an early shaper of something new,&quot the energy changed. People don’t want to be sold to. They want to be included.

Your brand isn't a logo. It’s a story told honestly enough that people want to join before the product is even finished.

We have 50 beta users now. 100+ conversations later, the product looks nothing like the first prompt I gave Claude.

If you’re building right now: What’s harder for you? The code, or getting people to care?
