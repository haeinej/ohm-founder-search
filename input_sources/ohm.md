# ohm.

ohm.

I think all the time. 

On the subway, in the shower, coming back home from events, at 1am. Thoughts arrive, some linger, most disappear. The few I hold onto, I try to share. But most of the time, they don't land. People around me — friends, classmates, coworkers — they nod. They move on. Not because they don't care. Because they are not the right minds for that thought.

I have felt that gap for most of my life. In Korea, I spent years inside systems that rewarded discipline and endurance. I was praised, and I mistook that praise for alignment. It was not until I left alone, at eighteen, for London with almost nothing that something shifted inside me. I wandered. I talked to strangers. 

People of different ages, backgrounds, worlds that would never have touched mine. Those conversations changed me more than any institution ever had.

I became more myself not through certainty or titles, but through encounter. Through hearing someone think in a way that made my own thinking visible to me for the first time.

That is the experience I cannot forget. That is the shock that constructed me, and is still reconstructing me. And it is almost impossible to find on purpose.

I want to bring more of these experiences to the world. 

I want people to discover themselves and the world through ohm.

Core Purpose.

People become more themselves through encounter with the right minds. 

Through the irreplaceable experience of thinking with someone whose mind meets yours; finding that your own thinking moves somewhere it could not have gone alone.

This has always been true. It will remain true long after any technology becomes obsolete. ohm. exists to make this encounter findable.

Mission.

Find the right minds for your thinking.

How it works.

You write down what you are thinking. Not a post. Not a performance. Just a thought — the kind that would otherwise stay in your head or disappear by morning.

ohm. finds the minds that resonate with that thought. Not based on who you are or who you know. Based on how you think.

It is a notebook that thinks back. You write into it the way you would write into a journal — but unlike a journal, it is alive. It surfaces other people's thoughts that meet yours. Sometimes those are thoughts you almost had yourself. Sometimes they are from an angle you never considered. And sometimes, a conversation starts from there that changes how you see everything.

Vision.

A thought written down by a software engineer midnight in San Francisco finds a designer in Seoul who has been circling the same idea from a completely different life. A conversation starts. Neither would have found the other through any existing system. Both leave thinking something they could not have thought alone.

The most meaningful intellectual relationships in a person's life are no longer limited by who they already know, where they went to school, or what city they live in. Anyone, anywhere, can find the minds that expand their thinking. That is the world ohm. is building toward.

I believe it because I have lived the opposite. I know what it is to be surrounded by people and still have nowhere for your thinking to go. I know what it is to meet one stranger who changes the direction of your life.

The most powerful technology for human thinking was never artificial. It was always another human.

ohm. finds that someone for you.
